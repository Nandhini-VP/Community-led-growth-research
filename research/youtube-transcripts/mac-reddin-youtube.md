# Mac Reddin — YouTube Transcript

## Video: Remote Talks Interview — Community-Led Growth & Commsor  
**Channel:** Remote Talks  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EeeM6yf9YSI  
**Date:** May 2021  

## Summary  
Mac Reddin, CEO of Commsor, explains how community-led growth works and why it's becoming a core go-to-market strategy for B2B SaaS companies.

## Key Insights

**1. Community ≠ Marketing**  
Community enables many-to-many relationships between users, unlike marketing which is one-directional. Treating community as a marketing channel limits its true potential.

**2. CNM over CRM**  
Commsor introduces the concept of a Community Network Manager (CNM), which connects all community touchpoints (Slack, forums, email, social) to give a unified view of engagement.

**3. Community takes time**  
Community building is a long-term investment. Like growing an apple tree, it requires consistent effort before becoming self-sustaining.

**4. Community of interest > Community of support**  
Modern communities are built around shared identity and interests, not just product usage. This makes them more engaging and scalable.

**5. Community touches every function**  
Community impacts multiple areas including sales, support, retention, and hiring, making it a cross-functional growth driver.

## My Take  
This reinforces that community-led growth is not a short-term acquisition tactic but a long-term business strategy. By focusing on relationships and shared identity, companies can build sustainable growth systems that support multiple functions across the organization.
