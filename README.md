## Why I Chose Community-Led Growth
 
Community-led growth (CLG) stood out to me as the most sustainable and differentiated GTM strategy among the options. While cold outreach and paid SEO are effective short-term, community builds a compounding asset — a group of engaged users who bring in more users, reduce churn, and generate authentic content. For B2B SaaS companies with limited budgets, CLG can replace or significantly reduce spend on paid acquisition.
 
I also chose this topic because it sits at the intersection of brand, product, and marketing — making it one of the most strategic areas a growth marketer can develop expertise in.

## How I Selected Experts
 
I applied three filters when choosing the 10 experts:
 
1. **Practitioner-first** — They have actually built communities that drove business growth, not just written about it.
2. **B2B relevance** — Their work is directly applicable to SaaS companies, not just consumer communities.
3. **Active content presence** — They publish regularly on LinkedIn, YouTube, Substack, or podcasts, making content collection meaningful and rich.
 
No expert was chosen just because they appeared first on Google.

## Insights & Takeaways
 
- Community is not a marketing channel — it's a business strategy
- The best communities are built around a shared identity or mission, not a product
- ROI from community is measurable through retention, referrals, and expansion revenue
- Community-led growth works best when product and community teams are aligned
- Early-stage communities must be founder/operator-led before they can scale
 
---

 ## My POV as a Growth Marketer

- Community-led growth is most effective for B2B SaaS products with repeat usage and strong peer-to-peer learning potential. 
- It is not a short-term acquisition channel, but a long-term strategy that compounds through retention and advocacy. 
- Companies attempting CLG must be willing to invest in consistent engagement and align product, marketing, and community teams early.
